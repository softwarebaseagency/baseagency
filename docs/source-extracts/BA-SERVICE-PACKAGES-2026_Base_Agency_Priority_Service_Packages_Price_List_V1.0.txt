SECTION 1 - PURPOSE
The purpose of this Priority Service Packages & Price List document is to give Base Agency a clear, professional and controlled pricing reference for the first stage of business development, client outreach, proposal preparation and sales conversion.
This document should be used by Sales, Client Relations, Finance, Operations and Management when discussing services, preparing proposals, creating quotations and presenting commercial packages to clients.
SECTION 2 - SCOPE
Social media management packages.
Printing and branding packages.
Website, business email and IT service packages.
Events management and production packages.
Interior design and fit-out packages.
CCTV, security and access control packages.
Combined monthly retainer packages.
Price control, quotation rules, payment terms and sales usage rules.
SECTION 3 - PRICING CONTROL NOTE
All prices in this document are internal recommended starting prices or price ranges. Final prices must be confirmed through an official quotation after reviewing client requirements, project scope, materials, supplier costs, location, timeline, urgency, taxes, delivery requirements and management approval.
No employee may promise a final price, discount, installment plan or special commercial term without approval from Management and Finance where required.

SECTION 14 - DISCOUNT APPROVAL RULES
Small discount up to 5% may be approved by Sales Manager or Management if margin is safe.
Discount between 5% and 10% requires Finance and Management review.
Discount above 10% requires CEO approval.
No discount is allowed if supplier cost, project cost or cash flow risk makes the project unprofitable.
Discount must be recorded in the quotation or proposal approval notes.
SECTION 15 - SALES USAGE RULES
Sales must use this document as a starting point, not as an unlimited final price list.
Sales must understand the client need before offering a package.
Final quotation must be prepared after scope clarification.
Operations must review delivery feasibility for technical or complex projects.
Finance must review payment terms, discounts and project cost where required.
Management must approve high-value, high-risk or strategic client offers.
