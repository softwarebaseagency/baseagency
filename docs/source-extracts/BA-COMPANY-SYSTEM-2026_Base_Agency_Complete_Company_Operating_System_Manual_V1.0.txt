SECTION 1 - PURPOSE
The purpose of this Complete Company Operating System Manual is to define how Base Agency shall operate as a structured, disciplined, controlled, and professional company.
This manual connects all company systems together, including management, sales, finance, human resources, operations, client experience, quality control, reporting, approvals, document control, and continuous improvement.
The goal is to ensure that Base Agency does not operate randomly, verbally, or without control. Every department, employee, client, project, payment, approval, task, and report must be managed through an official system.
SECTION 2 - COMPANY OPERATING PRINCIPLE
Base Agency shall operate based on the following principle:
• Every task must have an owner.
• Every client must be recorded.
• Every payment must be tracked.
• Every employee must have a file.
• Every project must have a code.
• Every decision must be documented.
• Every delay must be followed up.
• Every mistake must be corrected.
• Every department must report.
• Every month must be reviewed.

SECTION 4 - MANAGEMENT STRUCTURE
Base Agency shall be managed through a clear leadership structure.
CEO & Founder
The CEO is responsible for:
1. Final company direction.
2. Strategic decisions.
3. Major financial approvals.
4. High-value client decisions.
5. Senior management review.
6. Company growth and expansion.
7. Final approval of major policies, contracts, and systems.
COO / Operations Leadership
The COO is responsible for:
1. Daily company operations.
2. Project execution control.
3. Department coordination.
4. Staff follow-up.
5. Weekly management reporting.
6. Ensuring tasks are completed on time.
7. Reporting major issues to the CEO.
Department Responsible Persons
Each department or division must have a responsible person who manages:
1. Daily tasks.
2. Staff activity.
3. Department tracker updates.
4. Department problems.
5. Weekly reporting.
6. Implementation of company policies.

SECTION 6 - WEEKLY COMPANY ROUTINE
Base Agency shall hold a weekly management review meeting. Recommended day: Thursday. Recommended time: 04:00 PM. Chairperson: CEO / COO.
The weekly meeting shall review:
1. Sales performance.
2. Client follow-ups.
3. Proposals and quotations.
4. Finance status.
5. HR attendance and staff issues.
6. Active projects.
7. Delayed projects.
8. Client complaints or feedback.
9. Quality issues.
10. Risks and pending decisions.
11. Next week priorities.
All decisions and action items must be recorded in the Management Action Tracker.
SECTION 7 - MONTHLY COMPANY ROUTINE
At the end of each month, Base Agency shall prepare a monthly management report. The monthly report must be reviewed by management and approved by the CEO.
1. Executive summary.
2. Sales results.
3. Finance income, expenses, and profit/loss.
4. HR attendance and staff performance.
5. Operations and project performance.
6. Client experience summary.
7. Quality and risk summary.
8. KPI performance.
9. Problems and corrective actions.
10. Next month plan.
SECTION 8 - SALES OPERATING SYSTEM
The Sales System shall manage all potential clients from first contact to closed deal.
Sales System Components
1. Target client list.
2. CRM tracker.
3. Lead status.
4. Client communication log.
5. Meeting tracker.
6. Proposal tracker.
7. Quotation tracker.
8. Follow-up tracker.
9. Closed deal register.
10. Lost deal register.
Sales Workflow
1. Add client to target list.
2. Classify client by industry and priority.
3. Contact client by phone, WhatsApp, email, visit, or referral.
4. Record client response.
5. Schedule meeting if interested.
6. Identify client needs.
7. Send company profile or service package.
8. Prepare proposal or quotation.
9. Follow up.
10. Close deal or mark as lost.
11. If approved, hand over to Finance and Operations.
Sales Control Rules
1. No client shall be ignored after first contact.
2. Every interested client must have a follow-up date.
3. Every proposal must be recorded.
4. Every quotation must be recorded.
5. Every closed deal must be handed over formally.
6. Lost clients must include reason lost.
SECTION 9 - FINANCE OPERATING SYSTEM
The Finance System shall control all money entering and leaving Base Agency.
Finance System Components
1. Income tracker.
2. Expense tracker.
3. Invoice register.
4. Receipt register.
5. Payment request register.
6. Petty cash tracker.
7. Salary register.
8. Supplier payment register.
9. Monthly profit and loss summary.
10. Cash flow summary.
Finance Workflow
1. Client approval received.
2. Invoice prepared.
3. Payment received.
4. Receipt issued.
5. Income recorded.
6. Project finance status updated.
7. Expenses recorded with receipt.
8. Monthly finance report prepared.
Finance Control Rules
1. No income shall be unrecorded.
2. No expense shall be accepted without documentation.
3. No salary payment shall be made without salary record.
4. No supplier payment shall be made without approval.
5. Monthly profit/loss must be reviewed by CEO.
6. Finance data is confidential.

Operations System Components

3. Task assignment tracker.

10. Delivery handover register.

Operations Workflow

2. Project code created.
3. Scope reviewed.
4. Timeline prepared.
5. Tasks assigned.
6. Work begins.
7. Progress updated daily.
8. Quality check completed.
9. Client approval received.
10. Delivery handover completed.
11. Project closed.
Operations Control Rules
1. No project shall start without approved scope.
2. Every project must have a project code.
3. Every project must have a responsible person.
4. Every task must have a deadline.
5. Delays must be recorded.
6. Quality check is required before delivery.
7. Client approval must be recorded.
SECTION 12 - CLIENT EXPERIENCE SYSTEM
The Client Experience System shall ensure clients are treated professionally before, during, and after service delivery.

2. Client meeting notes.
3. Client needs analysis.
4. Complaint register.
5. Feedback register.
6. Satisfaction tracker.
7. Repeat client tracker.
8. Client retention tracker.
Client Experience Rules
1. Clients must receive professional communication.
2. Client messages must be answered on time.
3. Client requirements must be documented.
4. Complaints must be recorded and resolved.
5. Client approval must be documented.
6. Feedback must be collected after delivery.
7. Important clients must receive management attention.
SECTION 13 - QUALITY CONTROL SYSTEM
The Quality Control System shall ensure that all work delivered by Base Agency meets professional standards.
Quality control must apply to designs, social media content, printing work, websites, IT services, events, interior design, AV systems, security and CCTV projects, proposals, quotations, and official documents.
Quality Control Rules
1. Work must be checked before sending to client.
2. Important work must be reviewed by a responsible person.
3. Mistakes must be corrected before delivery.
4. Rework must be recorded.
5. Repeated mistakes must be reviewed by management.
6. Quality issues must have corrective action.

SECTION 15 - DOCUMENT CONTROL SYSTEM
Base Agency shall control all official documents through document codes, versions, approvals, and storage locations.
Document Control Rules
1. Every official document must have a document code.
2. Every document must have a title.
3. Every document must have a version number.
4. Every document must have an owner.
5. Every document must have a storage location.
6. Updated documents must replace old versions.
7. Old documents must be archived.
8. Approved documents must not be changed without permission.
Main Document Status Options
• Draft
• Under Review
• Approved
• Active
• Archived
• Replaced
• Cancelled
SECTION 16 - APPROVAL SYSTEM
Base Agency shall use a formal approval system to protect authority, accountability, finance, clients, employees, and company decisions.

[TABLE]

COO / Management Approval | Daily operational decisions; project task distribution; project timeline changes; internal workflow decisions; department coordination; routine client delivery issues.

SECTION 17 - COMMUNICATION SYSTEM
Base Agency shall use professional communication standards for all internal and external communication.
Internal Communication
Internal communication shall be clear, respectful, documented, and related to work. Approved channels include email, WhatsApp groups, internal memos, meetings, and task trackers.
External Communication
External communication with clients, suppliers, partners, and candidates must be professional. Official external communication must use company email, company WhatsApp number, official letterhead, approved proposal and quotation templates, and proper company signature.
SECTION 18 - IT, DATA & BACKUP SYSTEM
Base Agency shall protect company data and digital files. The IT system must include company email accounts, cloud storage, file backup, access control, password management, device tracking, antivirus/security protection, and website/domain management.
IT Control Rules
1. Company files must not be stored only on personal devices.
2. Important files must be backed up.
3. Access must be limited by role.
4. Passwords must not be shared casually.
5. Former employees must have access removed.
6. Confidential data must be protected.
SECTION 19 - RISK MANAGEMENT SYSTEM
Base Agency shall identify, record, and control risks. Risk categories include financial risk, client risk, project delay risk, HR risk, legal risk, IT and data risk, brand reputation risk, and operational risk.
Risk Control Rules
1. Major risks must be reported to management.
2. Every serious risk must have an action plan.
3. Delayed projects must be treated as risk.
4. Financial losses must be reviewed.
5. Client complaints must be monitored.
6. Risk status must be reviewed monthly.
SECTION 20 - BRAND & MARKETING SYSTEM
Base Agency shall maintain a consistent brand identity in all communication, proposals, social media, documents, uniforms, office materials, and presentations.
Brand control must include logo usage, color system, typography, social media style, proposal design, company profile design, staff email signature, and public communication tone. No public material should be published without review.
