1. Purpose of This Development Brief
This brief must be sent together with the official Base Agency operating system files. Its purpose is to guide the person or team who will build the digital system, dashboard, database, or internal platform for Base Agency.
The attached documents are not only reference files. They are the foundation for building a complete, live, professional, expandable, and controlled company operating system.
Main Purpose
1. Convert the attached Word documents into a live working system.
2. Create structured modules for management, sales, finance, HR, operations, clients, reports, approvals, and documents.
3. Allow authorized users to enter, update, filter, search, and review company data.
4. Create dashboards that show company performance clearly to management.
5. Reduce missing information, random follow-up, and undocumented decisions.
6. Make Base Agency operate through a real company operating system, not through scattered files or WhatsApp messages only.

3. Overall System Requirement
The required system must not be only a place to upload documents. It must be a live operating system where information is entered, updated, organized, searched, filtered, reported, and displayed through dashboards.
The system must allow Base Agency to:
1. Enter daily data easily from each department.
2. Track every client, employee, project, task, payment, expense, approval, and decision.
3. Show company status from one central management dashboard.
4. Generate weekly and monthly reports.
5. Control approval workflows and decision records.
6. Protect confidential HR, finance, client, and management data.
7. Assign responsibilities and deadlines.
8. Show delayed, pending, completed, and critical items clearly.
9. Use status colors: Green, Yellow, and Red.
10. Support future growth and additional branches or departments.

Sales Workflow
1. Add new client into the CRM or target client list.
2. Classify client by industry, service opportunity, and priority.
3. Contact client by phone, WhatsApp, email, Instagram, referral, or visit.
4. Record contact result and next follow-up date.
5. Schedule meeting if the client is interested.
6. Record client needs and expected service package.
7. Send company profile, proposal, quotation, or service package.
8. Follow up until the opportunity is won, lost, or postponed.
9. If won, hand over to Finance and Operations with complete details.

15. Filters, Search and Views
Every module must include useful filtering and search options. The system should make it easy for management and users to find records quickly.
- Filter by date range.
- Filter by department or service division.
- Filter by status.
- Filter by priority.
- Filter by responsible person.
- Filter by client name.
- Filter by project code, invoice code, employee code, or document code.
- Search by keyword in notes or descriptions.
- Saved views for CEO, Sales, Finance, HR, Operations, and Management.

18. Questions the Developer Must Answer Before Starting
1. What platform do you recommend for the system and why?
2. Will the system be web-based, Excel-based, cloud-based, or custom software?
3. Can multiple users enter data at the same time?
4. Can access be limited by user role?
5. Can the system generate reports automatically?
6. Can data be exported to Excel and PDF?
7. Can documents and attachments be uploaded to each record?
8. How will data backup be handled?
9. How will confidential HR and finance information be protected?
10. How long will development, testing, and training take?
