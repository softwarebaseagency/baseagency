SECTION 1 - PURPOSE
The purpose of this Operations, Projects & Task Management Tracker is to help Base Agency plan, organize, assign, monitor, deliver, and close all client projects and internal operational tasks.
This tracker shall be used to control project status, task assignments, deadlines, responsible persons, service divisions, client requirements, project progress, issues, quality checks, approvals, handovers, and final project closure.
SECTION 2 - OPERATIONS OBJECTIVES
1. Record all approved projects.
2. Assign project responsibilities clearly.
3. Track project start dates and deadlines.
4. Track task assignments for employees and divisions.
5. Monitor project progress.
6. Identify delays and problems early.
7. Control quality before delivery.
8. Record client approvals and handovers.
9. Close projects professionally.
10. Prepare weekly and monthly operations reports.

SECTION 5 - PROJECT STATUS DEFINITIONS

[TABLE]
Status | Meaning
Not Started | Project is approved but work has not started
In Progress | Work is currently active
Waiting Client | Waiting for client information, files, approval, or feedback
Waiting Internal Approval | Waiting for internal review or management approval
Delayed | Project has missed deadline or risk of delay exists
Quality Check | Work is under internal quality review
Ready for Delivery | Work is approved internally and ready to deliver
Delivered | Work has been delivered to client
Completed | Project work is completed
Closed | Project is completed, approved, recorded, and archived
Cancelled | Project was cancelled

SECTION 22 - DAILY OPERATIONS ROUTINE
Daily operations tasks:
1. Review active projects.
2. Review tasks due today.
3. Assign new tasks.
4. Follow up with responsible persons.
5. Update project progress.
6. Record delays and issues.
7. Check client approvals required.
8. Review completed work.
9. Prepare tomorrow’s task plan.
SECTION 23 - WEEKLY OPERATIONS ROUTINE
Weekly operations tasks:
1. Review all active projects.
2. Review project deadlines.
3. Review delayed tasks.
4. Review quality check status.
5. Review client approval status.
6. Review project handovers from Sales.
7. Review finance/payment dependencies.
8. Prepare weekly operations report.
9. Submit report to Management.
SECTION 24 - MONTHLY OPERATIONS ROUTINE
Monthly operations tasks:
1. Review all projects started during the month.
2. Review all completed projects.
3. Review project profitability with Finance where required.
4. Review project delays and reasons.
5. Review client satisfaction and feedback.
6. Review staff workload.
7. Review quality issues.
8. Prepare monthly operations report.
9. Prepare improvement actions for next month.
SECTION 25 - SALES TO OPERATIONS HANDOVER CONTROL
When Sales closes a deal, Operations must receive:
1. Client name.
2. Contact person.
3. Approved proposal or quotation.
4. Approved scope.
5. Approved price.
6. Payment status.
7. Deadline.
8. Files and materials received.
9. Client special requirements.
10. Follow-up notes.
No project should start without clear scope, deadline, and responsibility.
SECTION 26 - OPERATIONS CONTROL RULES
The following control rules are mandatory:
1. Every project must have a project code.
2. Every project must have a responsible project manager.
3. Every task must have a responsible person.
4. Every task must have a deadline.
5. Delays must be recorded.
6. Issues must be reported early.
7. Quality check must be completed before delivery.
8. Client approvals must be documented.
9. Project delivery must be recorded.
10. Project closure must be completed before archiving.
11. Operations tracker must be updated daily.
12. Weekly operations report must be prepared.
